Q: What documents do I need for a Georgia driver's license?
A: You'll need one document proving identity (such as a birth certificate, valid US passport, or certificate of naturalization), your Social Security card, and two documents proving Georgia residency (such as a utility bill, bank statement, or lease agreement). Visit https://dds.georgia.gov for the full list of acceptable documents.

Q: How long does a Georgia driver's license last?
A: A standard Georgia driver's license is valid for 8 years. You can renew up to 150 days before the expiration date.

Q: What are Georgia DDS office hours?
A: Most Georgia DDS Customer Service Centers are open Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 6:30 PM. Some locations may have different hours. Check your nearest location at https://dds.georgia.gov/locations/customer-service-centers.

Q: What is SNAP / food stamps?
A: SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program) is a federal program that provides monthly benefits to help low-income households buy food. In Georgia, SNAP is administered by the Division of Family and Children Services (DFCS). Benefits are loaded onto a Georgia EBT card each month.

Q: How do I apply for SNAP in Georgia?
A: You can apply online through Georgia Gateway at https://gateway.ga.gov/, in person at your local DFCS office, or by mailing a completed application. You'll need to provide proof of identity, income, and residency.

Q: How long does SNAP approval take in Georgia?
A: Applications are typically processed within 30 days. If you have very low income and resources, you may qualify for expedited processing within 7 days.

Q: What can I buy with SNAP benefits?
A: SNAP benefits can be used to buy most food items including fruits, vegetables, meat, dairy, bread, cereals, and seeds or plants that produce food. You cannot use SNAP to buy alcohol, tobacco, vitamins, prepared hot foods, or non-food items like household supplies.

Q: Where do I find my nearest DFCS office?
A: You can find your nearest Division of Family and Children Services (DFCS) office by visiting https://dfcs.georgia.gov/locations or by calling 1-877-423-4746.
